Trees Growth

Recent research from 2025 underscores the indispensable role of trees as sophisticated "biogenic cooling" systems, highlighting their ability to regulate microclimates through transpiration and shade.

To put their power into perspective, the cooling effect of a single healthy tree is equivalent to the power of two standard air conditioning units running for 20 hours a day.

In densely populated "urban heat islands," where asphalt and concrete trap heat, the strategic expansion of tree growth is a primary tool for climate resilience; urban canopies have been shown to reduce surface temperatures by up to 8Â°C. This natural refrigeration occurs because trees do more than just block sunlight; they actively release water vapor into the air, a process that consumes heat energy and provides a sustainable, low-energy alternative to mechanical cooling.
